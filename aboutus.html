Our Patron, St. Francis Xavier.

St. Francis Higher Secondary School of which the motto is “ All for the glory of God” is named after St. Francis Xavier, the great educationalist and the great missionary saint who lived for the glory of God. Though a saint of the 16th Century, St. Francis Xavier is a model for all educationalists in any age and time.

While working as a Professor at the University of Paris, he encountered Ignatius of Loyola, who is said to have posed the question from Mk.8:36, "What will it profit a man to gain the whole world, and lose his own soul?” Though at first hesitant, Francis was eventually inspired by his friend's example. On August 15, 1534, in the Montmartre section of Paris, Francis Xavier, Loyola and five others for the sake of the glory of God, pledged themselves to the Society of Jesus (the Jesuits).

Much of his life was spent for the glory of God, tending to missions in areas such as India and Japan. He was admired for his ability to live and work side by side with the poor of God. He was gifted with the real miracle of tongues and he used the gift of tongue for the greater glory of God. Francis was 46 when he died on China's Shangchuan Island on December 3, 1552.Xavier was beatified by Pope Paul V in 1619, and canonized by Pope Gregory XV in 1622. A famed missionary himself, he is now the patron saint of missionaries and education being a great educationalist who lived for the glory of God alone
