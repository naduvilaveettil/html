Co-Foundresses

St. Francis Higher Secondary School is the consequence of a visualization, the kernels of which was planted years ago by Mother Eliswa, the foundress; Mother Anna and Mother Thresia the co-foundresses of the Congregation of Teresian Carmelites when they founded the first indigenous congregation on February13, 1866 at Koonammavu, Kerala.

Mother Thresia was appointed as the first headmistress and Mother Eliswa as the first Manager of the first ever temporary school which the congregation anticipated for educating girls. Under the gifted guidance of Mother Thresia, who did everything for the greater glory of God, the enrolled wrote the first words ‘Jesus and God’. Religious instruction was an integral part of their education. The students were given training in reading scriptural texts. Parents were eager to send their children to this first school. As the number of students increased, an incredible progress in the education scheme sprout forth gradually under the auspicious guidance of the Foundress Mother Eliswa and co-foundress Mother Thresia until it is grown into High schools, Higher Secondary schools and colleges throughout India.

The first headmistress Mother Thresia and the first Manager Mother Eliswa firmly believed that women are the indispensable part of a society wherein at that time they were considered as mere marginal and good for nothing. The development of future generation mainly depends upon the education of women section and so it is realized to be the most essential part for the development of the society. Mother Thresia was of the opinion that mother of child is the first educator of the child. From birth to school age, the influence of mother on the child is important. Most of the time a child lives in contact with its mother and is influenced and affected by her. Therefore it is very necessary that a mother shall be imbued with good qualities and shall act as a guide. Thus the education of women is of great importance in our national and social life .

There is a Chinese saying, “If you wish to plan for a year, plant wheat, if you wish to plan for 10 years, grow trees, if you wish to plan for 100 years educate your women.” This indicates the importance of women education. Napoleon said, “ Give me an educated mother, I shall promise you the birth of a civilized nation.” By educating the women we can educate the whole nation.

A woman for God and a woman for others, Mother Thresia was blessed with the predominant qualities like genuine courage, magnanimity, warmth of love, cheerfulness and exemplary comportment who did everything for the greater glory of God. She was talented, outgoing, adaptable, affectionate, courageous, enthusiastic, wise, intelligent, a mystic yet an energetic performer and all the more totally human. She was a holy woman, a womanly woman. She was both a contemplative and an active educationalist. She was a visionary, an ideal leader, a heroic sufferer for God and is a SAINT, her motto being “I am entirely yours, Oh my God.”
